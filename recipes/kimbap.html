Recipe for Kimbap

Description

Kimbap (also spelled "gimbap") is a popular and beloved Korean dish known for its delicious combination of flavors, vibrant colors, and portable nature. Often referred to as the Korean version of sushi or a sushi roll, kimbap is a versatile and customizable dish that has become a staple in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 4 dried seaweed sheets
  • 4.2 ounces of spinach
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 4.2 ounces of carrots, julienned
  • 2 to 4 imitation crab sticks, cut in half length ways
  • 4 sticks of BBQ kimbap ham, cut into long strips
  • 4 yellow radish pickle, cut into long strips
  • 12 strips of seasoned edible burdock root
  • 2.5 cups of cooked short grain rice
  • 2 Tbsp of sesame oil
  • 3/8 tsp of fine sea salt

Steps

  1. Rinse the spinach and parboil it in rapidly boiling water for 30 seconds. Drain the water and add 1/8 tsp salt and 1/2 Tbsp sesame oil and mix well
  2. Add a small dash of cooking oil to a heated pan, then add the beaten eggs and cook both sides well over medium heat. After cooking, cut into long strips
  3. In a heated pan, lightly cook the carrots, crab sticks, and kimbap ham for 1 min per ingredient, separately, and over medium heat
  4. Add 1/2 Tbsp sesame oil and 1/4 tsp fine sea salt to the rice and mix well
  5. Place one dried seaweed sheet on a bamboo mat then spread the rice evenly and thinly to cover about 2/3 of the seaweed
  6. Fill the seaweed sheet with the prepped ingredients and roll it up. Use water to make the seaweed stick
  7. Paste sesame oil on top of the roll and then slice it into bite size pieces
