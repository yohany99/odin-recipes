Recipe for Jjajangmyeon

Description

Jjajangmyeon, a popular Korean-Chinese noodle dish, is a savory and satisfying culinary delight that combines tender noodles with a rich, dark, and slightly sweet black bean sauce. This beloved dish has become an integral part of Korean cuisine, cherished for its unique flavors and comforting qualities.

Ingredients

  • Jjajangmyeon noodles
  • 1/2 pound of pork belly, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup of Korean radish, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup of zucchini, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup of potato, peeled and cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1.5 cups of onion chunks
  • 3 Tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup and 1 Tbsp of chunjang
  • 2 Tbsp of potato starch powder, combined with 1/4 cup of water and 1 tsp of sugar
  • 1 tsp of toasted sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup of cucumber, cut into thin matchsticks
  • Water

Steps

  1. Stir-fry the pork belly in a large, deep wok with 1 Tbsp of vegetable oil for about 4-5 minutes
  2. Pour out the excess pork fat
  3. Add radish and stir-fry for 1 minute
  4. Add potato, onion, and zucchini and keep stirring for about 3 minutes until the potato looks a little translucent
  5. Clear a space in the center of the wok
  6. Add 2 Tbsp of vegetable oil to the center, then add 1/4 cup of chunjang. Stir for 1 minute, then mix everything together and keep stirring
  7. Add 2 cups of water to the wok and let it simmer with the lid closed for about 10 minutes
  8. Stir in the starch water little by little
  9. Add sesame oil and remove from the heat
  10. Serve with the jjajangmyeon noodles
