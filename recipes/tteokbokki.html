Recipe for Tteokbokki

Description

Tteokbokki is a beloved and iconic Korean street food that tantalizes the taste buds with its spicy, chewy, and satisfying qualities. This popular snack or comfort food has a rich history and a prominent place in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of cylinder shaped rice cake
  • 4 cups of water
  • 7 large anchovies, with heads and intestines removed
  • 6 x 8 inch dried kelp
  • 1/3 cup of gochujang
  • 1 Tbsp of gochugaru
  • 1 Tbsp of sugar
  • 3 green onions, cut into 3 inch long pieces
  • 2 hard boiled eggs
  • 1/2 pound of fish cakes

Steps

  1. Add the water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp to a shallow pot or pan. Boil for 15 minutes over medium-high heat
  2. Combine gochujang, gochugaru, and sugar in a small bowl
  3. Remove the anchovies and kelp from the pot and add the rice cakes, the mixture, green onion, fish cakes, and hard boiled eggs
  4. Stir gently when it starts to boil. Let it simmer and keep stirring until the rice cake turns soft and the sauce thickens
